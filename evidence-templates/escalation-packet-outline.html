Escalation Packet Outline

This is the minimum structure the presenter should be able to narrate on Page 4 and Page 5 without reopening any design decisions during a live walkthrough.

Section 1

Incident Summary

Name the scenario, the current severity, the affected entity, and why the issue is still active rather than merely interesting.

Section 2

Proof Chain

List the one strongest event, the one strongest rule or query, and the one cross-source link that turns separate signals into one incident.

Section 3

Entities in Scope

Show the user, asset, secret, database, or bucket that matters, plus the one entity relationship that most changes urgency.

Section 4

Business Impact

Translate the telemetry into operational risk: privilege expansion, protected secret exposure, sensitive data access, or service-impacting service disruption.

Section 5

Immediate Actions

Capture the first containment move, the next proof target, and which team needs to act if the escalation is accepted.

Section 6

Decision Ask

End with a clean recommendation: monitor, contain, escalate, or invoke incident response. Avoid leaving the packet without an owner and timeframe.

Escalation quality comes from continuity, not from having the most screenshots. Minimum live standard: one event proof + one rule/query proof + one operator decision.
